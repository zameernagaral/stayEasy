Module 1

The 5 Stages of Design Thinking

The d.school model consists of five modes. Remember: this is an iterative process, not a strictly linear one. Click on each stage below to explore its goals and tools.

Core Principles

  • Human-Centric: Focuses on people (employees, customers).
  • Collaborative: Requires diverse teams and viewpoints.
  • Iterative: It is a cycle of prototyping, testing, and refining.
  • Action-Oriented: "Do" rather than just "Think".

Strategy & IT Integration

How Design Thinking merges with Business Process Management, Agile, and Strategic Innovation.

Types of Innovation

Understanding the difference between market impact and technology change.

Note: Hover over the quadrants to see examples. Radical innovation changes both technology and business models, while Incremental improves existing ones.

Goal: Align operations with strategy, improve communication, consistency, and control.

DT's Role: Moves BPM beyond simple maps to being customer-centric and empathy-driven.

Core Value: Individuals and interactions over processes and tools. Iterative & incremental.

Relationship: Design Thinking helps find the right problems to solve; Agile efficiently solves them.

Key Elements: Transparency, Culture of Courage (safe to fail), Strong Communication, Work-Life Balance.

Context: Essential for modern distributed teams working across time zones and cultures.

  • Goal: Competitive advantage & growth.
  • Scope: Managed innovation, Strategic alignment, Industry foresight.
  • DT Integration: Reviewing data, Simulating experiences, Conversing on strategy, Collaborating on solutions.
